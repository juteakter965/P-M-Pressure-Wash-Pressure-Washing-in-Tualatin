What is Pressure Washing?

Pressure washing, also known as power washing, utilizes high-pressure water spray to remove dirt, grime, mold, mildew, algae, and other contaminants from surfaces including buildings, vehicles, and concrete. The process employs specialized equipment that delivers water at pressures ranging from 750 to 30,000 pounds per square inch (PSI), effectively cleaning surfaces that would be difficult or impossible to clean through traditional methods.

Understanding Pressure Washing vs. Power Washing

While many people use these terms interchangeably, there is a technical distinction between pressure washing and power washing. Pressure washing uses ambient temperature water delivered at high pressure, while power washing incorporates a heating element that produces hot water. The heated water in power washing makes it particularly effective for removing stubborn substances like oil, grease, and sticky residues. However, for most residential and commercial applications in Tualatin, standard pressure washing with cold water proves both effective and safer for delicate surfaces.

‚Ñπ Professional Equipment Makes the Difference

Professional-grade pressure washing equipment delivers consistent water pressure and flow rates that consumer-grade machines cannot match. Commercial units feature adjustable pressure settings, various nozzle configurations, superior water heating capabilities, and robust construction designed for frequent, demanding use. This professional equipment ensures thorough cleaning while protecting your property from damage that can occur with improper equipment or technique.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pressure Washing in Tualatin

How often should I have my Tualatin property pressure washed?

Most Tualatin residential properties benefit from annual pressure washing, typically in late spring or early summer. However, properties with significant tree cover, north-facing surfaces, or areas prone to moss growth may require service twice yearly. Commercial properties with high foot traffic often need quarterly cleaning to maintain appearance and safety. Your specific maintenance schedule depends on your property's exposure to elements, surrounding environment, and aesthetic standards.

Will pressure washing damage my siding, deck, or roof?

When performed by trained professionals using appropriate pressure levels and techniques, pressure washing is safe for all standard building materials. Reputable companies adjust pressure based on surface type‚Äîusing lower pressure for delicate materials like wood siding, painted surfaces, and roofing, and higher pressure for durable surfaces like concrete and brick. Soft washing techniques, which use low pressure combined with specialized cleaning solutions, safely clean sensitive surfaces without risk of damage.

What is the best time of year for pressure washing in Tualatin?

In Tualatin's climate, late spring through early fall provides optimal conditions for pressure washing. Warmer temperatures and lower humidity during these months allow surfaces to dry quickly, reducing the risk of mold or mildew regrowth. However, professional services operate year-round and can accommodate your schedule regardless of season. Winter services require special precautions to prevent freezing, but experienced companies handle these considerations routinely.

How long does pressure washing take?

Service duration varies significantly based on property size and scope of work. A typical single-family home exterior wash takes 2-4 hours, while driveway cleaning might require only 1-2 hours. Larger projects like commercial building cleaning or comprehensive property services may take a full day or require multiple visits. Professional companies provide time estimates during the quoting process.

Do I need to be home during pressure washing service?

For exterior cleaning, you typically don't need to be present once you've discussed the scope of work and approved the service plan. Many homeowners prefer to be available to answer questions and review completed work, but it's not required. If your property has locked gates or other access considerations, coordinate these details in advance. Commercial services are often scheduled during off-hours to minimize disruption to business operations.

Are pressure washing cleaning solutions safe for plants and pets?

Reputable pressure washing companies use biodegradable, eco-friendly cleaning solutions that are safe for landscaping and pets when properly diluted and applied. Professional technicians take care to protect delicate plants by pre-wetting them, covering sensitive species, and thoroughly rinsing all treated areas. However, it's advisable to keep pets indoors during service and allow cleaning solutions to fully rinse away before allowing pets back into treated areas.

Can pressure washing remove moss from my roof?

Yes, professional pressure washing effectively removes moss, algae, and lichen from roofs using specialized soft washing techniques. Unlike high-pressure washing that can damage shingles, soft washing uses low pressure (typically under 500 PSI) combined with cleaning solutions specifically formulated to kill and remove organic growth. This method safely cleans roofs while protecting roofing materials and extending their lifespan. Many companies offer preventive treatments that inhibit future moss growth.

Will pressure washing remove oil stains from my driveway?

Professional pressure washing can significantly reduce or eliminate most oil stains from concrete driveways and garage floors. The process typically involves pre-treating stains with specialized degreasers or hot water before high-pressure washing. While very old or deeply penetrated stains may not completely disappear, professional treatment produces dramatic improvement in most cases. For stubborn stains, some companies offer additional treatments or surface restoration services.

Should I pressure wash before painting or staining?

Yes, pressure washing is essential preparation before painting or staining exterior surfaces. Proper cleaning removes dirt, mildew, loose paint, and other contaminants that prevent good adhesion. Professional pressure washing also reveals underlying surface problems that should be addressed before applying new finishes. Most painting contractors require surfaces to be pressure washed and fully dried before they begin work, making it a critical first step in any exterior painting or staining project.
